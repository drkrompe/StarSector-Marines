## Notes / decisions made

- **Audit-first approach was the right call.** The Explore agent's
  pipeline survey changed the slice plan shape from "binary + ternary
  in one slice" to "polish → extract → ternary → emission". Saved a
  refactor-mid-stream pivot when the critique flagged the two-field
  contract. Recorded the pattern as a preferred approach for non-trivial
  refactors that span unfamiliar code.
- **Distance-indexed labels (vs explicit-side fields)** were the
  critique's main DESIGN finding. Adopted in the polish commit;
  generalizes cleanly from binary `[THRONE, ENTRY]` to ternary
  `[THRONE, INNER, ENTRY]` without changing the config shape.
- **Three-chamber design has an open anchor-placement question** —
  BFS-from-center would land in the middle chamber if all three are
  equal-sized; the distance-from-anchor labeling means whichever
  chamber the anchor lands in becomes THRONE. Resolution baked into
  Slice C plan: ternary strategy carves an **asymmetric** chamber
  layout (middle is narrowest) so the center falls in an end chamber.
- **Corridors as first-class structure are out of scope.** Station
  fills will likely need a different orchestrator entirely (corridors-
  as-primary, rooms-as-vertices) rather than retrofitting BSP.
  Documented in
  [`../mapgen/pipeline-audit.md`](../mapgen/pipeline-audit.md) §
  "Corridors — the bigger blocker for stations" so the next designer
  doesn't accidentally bake BSP-only geometry into `PartitionStrategy`.

## Process observation

The slice cadence felt right this session — slice + background
critique (parallel) + polish + commit, with the audit done up front
informing the slice shape. The single biggest payoff was catching the
two-field-contract design issue in the critique BEFORE Slice B
landed, because the polish commit was free; if I'd shipped Slice B
on the original contract and only then caught the issue, I'd have
had to either revert Slice B or layer the polish on top of more
churn.

Will continue this rhythm into Slice B: ship the extraction, fire a
critique in background, polish on findings, then move into Slice C.
