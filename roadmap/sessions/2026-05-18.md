## Functional changes

### Slice 2 (Stories K + J)

- **Custom-plan escape hatch.** Added `Goal.customPlan(squad, sim)`
  returning null by default ‚Äî when non-null, replaces the backward-
  chaining planner's output entirely. Both `SecureObjectiveZone` and
  `CordonForPlant` use it; the action set they emit isn't reachable by
  precondition chains (BFS-derived zone paths, per-portal guard cells)
  so the standard planner couldn't compose them anyway.
- **Per-zone parameterized actions.** `EnterZone(zoneId, destX, destY)`
  and `ClearZone(zoneId)` carry their target zone as construction
  state. Not registered in `INFANTRY_ACTIONS` ‚Äî only `SecureObjectiveZone`'s
  customPlan emits them.
- **Cordon discipline by positioning.** `HoldPortalCordon` puts each
  holder on a cell inside the planter's zone, facing their assigned
  doorway. Holders fire on anything in LoS arc, but don't peel off the
  post to chase ‚Äî that's what makes the cordon read on screen.
- **Mutual-exclusion-by-relevance** between `SecureObjectiveZone` (fires
  when planter zone != squad zone) and `CordonForPlant` (fires when
  planter zone == squad zone). Smooth handoff: marines push into the
  charge room, then the goal flips and they spread to the doorways.

### FireStance

- New `FireStance` enum (`STANCED` 1.0√ó, `MOVING` 0.5√ó) ‚Äî applied as a
  multiplier on `accuracy` inside `InfantryWeapons.fireShot`.
- Replaces the prior "fireOpportunistically is a separate concept"
  thinking. Suppression-on-the-move is just normal fire with a stance
  modifier. STANCED = stopped + firing; MOVING = mid-step + firing.
- Burst continuation reads `FireStance.stanceFor(unit)` ‚Äî heuristic
  based on `moveProgress > 0`. Explicit callsites in actions pass the
  intended stance (EnterZone always MOVING during transit; cordon
  holders STANCED when on post, MOVING in transit; KitRetriever MOVING
  while running to a kit).

### PlanterBehavior retirement

- `PlanterBehavior.java` deleted. The planter slot now lives inside
  `HoldPortalCordon` as the `"planter"` slot ‚Äî same channel-to-charge-
  cell-then-dwell shape, but driven by the squad plan instead of a
  per-unit dispatch. Unit keeps `role=PLANTER` so `ChargeSiteObjective`
  still finds it.
- Fixes "planter walks straight through enemies to the charge site"
  failure mode: now the planter waits inside the squad plan, dwells
  in the cordon room until the cordon is set, and then channels.

### Story B (squad morale)

- **`Squad.morale`** ‚Äî float in [0, 1], starts at 1.0.
- **Drain hooks** in `BattleSimulation.applyDamage`:
  - `-0.05` (`MORALE_DROP_ON_HIT`) per hit on a squadmate.
  - `+ -0.30` (`MORALE_DROP_ON_DEATH`) additional drain when the hit
    kills.
- **Recovery pass** (`updateSquadMorale`, runs between
  `updateSquadAlertLevels` and `updateSquadFallback`):
  - `+0.20/sec` (`MORALE_RECOVERY_RATE √ó TICK_DT`) when
    `!_engagedThisTick` (no LoS to any enemy this tick).
  - Cap = `aliveMembers / originalSize`. A 4‚Üí2 squad caps at 0.5;
    further casualties tighten the cap to 0.25 and the squad is
    effectively retired.
  - Hysteresis: `moraleBroken` flips true below 0.3
    (`MORALE_BROKEN_THRESHOLD`), clears above 0.5
    (`MORALE_CLEAR_THRESHOLD`). Prevents flickering at the boundary.
- **`AirSystem` ratchets `originalSize`** as marines deboard (defenders
  already get it stamped in `BattleSetup`). Adds `AirSimContext.getSquad(int)`
  so the deboard pass can find the freshly-minted squad.
- **`Predicate.MORALE_BROKEN`** evaluator reads `squad.moraleBroken`.
  `Predicate.SQUAD_BELOW_HALF_STRENGTH` is `@Deprecated` and stubbed to
  `STUB_FALSE` ‚Äî kept in the enum so any external readers don't fail
  the load-time predicate-count check, but the eval flips it false so
  it can't silently drive behavior.
- **`SurviveContact`** lives in the `SURVIVAL` priority bucket.
  Relevance reads `MORALE_BROKEN`. customPlan emits a single
  `BreakContact` step.
- **`BreakContact`** picks a cover cell via
  `TacticalScoring.findFallbackPosition` (same primitive the legacy
  per-unit fall-back used), caches on `Unit.fallbackCellX/Y`, paths
  there with MOVING-stance opportunistic fire, and on arrival holds +
  fires STANCED. Cached destination is **sticky once arrived** ‚Äî won't
  churn if LoS opens up; the squad-replan cycle (2s) re-evaluates.

### Legacy fall-back gate (Story B followup)

- `BattleSimulation.rollFallbackOnHit` early-skips when
  `target.squadId != Unit.NO_SQUAD && target.mech == null` ‚Äî
  GOAP-driven infantry never enters the legacy per-unit fall-back
  state. Mechs (until their GOAP lands) and civilians (`NO_SQUAD`,
  used by `FleeBehavior`) keep the legacy roll.

## Notes / accepted tradeoffs

- **Squad morale tuning is placeholder.** `0.05 / 0.30 / 0.20 / sec`
  drain/death/recovery rates and `0.3 / 0.5` thresholds are the
  starting point; user confirmed they feel right in initial playtest.
  Easy single-constant tweaks if anything reads wrong later.
- **`Unit.fallbackCellX/Y` is dual-use during the transition.** Both
  `BreakContact` (GOAP infantry retreat destination) and
  `FallbackBehavior` (mech + civilian legacy timer) write to these
  fields. The gate in `rollFallbackOnHit` keeps them from clashing on
  the same unit at the same time. When mech GOAP lands and
  `FallbackBehavior` retires, the dual-use collapses naturally.
- **`@Deprecated SQUAD_BELOW_HALF_STRENGTH` enum entry retained.**
  Removing the enum slot would shift later predicate ordinals and
  invalidate any in-flight `WorldState` instances that captured those
  bits (in tests; the sim itself rebuilds them every replan). Cheaper
  to leave it stubbed than to renumber.
- **`originalSize` ratchets per marine deboard.** A shuttle dropping 4
  marines bumps `originalSize` to 4 as the 4th deboards. If a shuttle
  is shot down mid-deboard, the squad's `originalSize` reflects the
  marines that actually landed ‚Äî that's the correct read of "how many
  showed up to this fight."
- **The pre-existing `NatureZonePreviewTest` failure cleared up on
  re-run.** Tileset/manifest sync ‚Äî looks transient (cached artifact
  mismatch). Worth eyeing on the next full build if it flares again.

## Personal note

The cleanest take from this session: Story B's original "alive count
threshold" trigger was a *trap I was about to set*. The user spotted
that monotonic predicates make for one-way-door goals ‚Äî and the morale
variable that came out of it is the right shape not just for retreat,
but as the substrate for any future "this squad is rattled" signal.
The cap-decays-with-casualties detail is what gives the simulation
weight: a fully fresh squad can take a punch and re-enter; a mauled
squad can't shake it off no matter how long they hide. That maps to
real fireteam doctrine without needing to author it. Cheap mechanic,
expensive-looking outcome.

The parked design docs (12 + 13) are the other quiet win. We have a
clear ordering ‚Äî Slice 3 (cover) OR commander tier next, mech GOAP
after commander ‚Äî and the deferred work has homes the next session
can walk into cold.
