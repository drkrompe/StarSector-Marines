# 2026-05-20 — Mech morale Stage 2: per-chassis morale + MechSurviveContact

Pulled forward `roadmap/ai/14-mech-stage1.md`'s "Mech survival" Stage 2
work after a playtest dump (squad_0) showed a fully morale-broken mech
squad still pursuing `MechEliminateEnemies`. Root cause: Stage 1 mechs
were explicitly "implacable" — the legacy `rollFallbackOnHit` skips all
GOAP squad members and `MECH_GOALS` had no `SurviveContact` analog. The
MISSION-tier role goals already had the `MORALE_BROKEN` carve-out (left
behind from a prior session that saw the same dump), but with no
SURVIVAL-tier mech goal to receive the handoff, the planner just fell
through to `MechEliminateEnemiesGoal`.

## Design notes

**HP-threshold drain, not per-hit.** Per the roadmap. A fresh mech taking
chip damage shouldn't bleed morale — it bleeds morale at chassis
milestones (75% → 50% → 25% → 10% HP). Monotonic via
`MechLoadoutState.hpThresholdsCrossed` so a (hypothetical future)
self-heal can't refund drains.

**Armor-gone cap as HP fraction.** No armor system exists today — the
roadmap's "cap when armor is gone" became "cap when HP < 50% maxHp".
Below that, morale ceiling drops to 0.50 (and clear threshold to
0.40 × 0.50 = 0.20). Combined with monotonic drain, this is the
load-bearing piece for "wounded mech locked in BreakContact for the rest
of the fight."

**Squad aggregation = majority broken.** GOAP operates at squad level
today; per-member goal assignment via the role assigner would let only
broken mechs retreat while others stay engaging, but the simpler
"squad-wide BreakContact when half are broken" matches infantry semantics
and is what the planner can act on cleanly. Refinement candidate once
playtest motivates it.

**Per-mech `timeSinceUnderFire`.** Each chassis gates its own recovery —
a mech that ducked behind cover while a squadmate took chip can compose
while the squadmate keeps the recovery timer pinned for itself. Different
shape than the squad-wide infantry timer.

## What I deliberately didn't do

- **Per-member goal routing.** Action declares `requiredMembers=1` and
  takes everyone in the default "any" slot. Could split broken vs
  unbroken into separate role slots — deferred until playtest shows
  whole-squad-retreats reading wrong.
- **Mech morale UI/dump.** The `SquadStateDumper` reads `squad.moraleBroken`
  which is now correctly aggregated, but doesn't expose per-mech morale.
  Worth adding when the next dump-driven debugging session needs it.
- **Tuning pass.** Numbers picked from the roadmap table; one playtest
  loop should validate before further changes.
